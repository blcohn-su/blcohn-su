Life at Syracuse University...

My top 5 favorite things about Syracuse University:

  1. Every member of the S.U. family bleeds orange. Whether its at a basketball game or on the quad, members of the Syracuse community are always showing their orange pride.
  2. The different colleges within the university allow for a more engaging education, catered to the interests of each student. Additionally, the colleges provide for a smaller environment within the large campus which allows students to feel more comfortable.
  3. Syracuse University is undeniably beautiful. The architecture of each building is breathtaking. My favorite time of the year on campus is during the fall when the leaves start to change color, making the campus even more beautiful.
  4. Another thing amazing thing about Syracuse is the alumni network. S.U. alumni are eager to hire recent graduates as well as provide them with invaluable career advice.
  5. Lastly, Syracuse Unviersity provides its students with numerous extracurriculars. Whatever it is a student is interested in doing, there are resources to help join a club or organization or begin their own.
 

Some of My Favorite Classes I've Taken at S.U.:

  • PRL214: Writing for News and Public Relations in the Digital Age.
    • I enjoyed this class because it provided me with hands-on experience in writing for public relations. While learning from a textbook is valuable, the opportunity to write allowed me to grow exponentially.
  • IST263: Intro to Front-End Web Development
    • In this class, I learned the basics of the HTML/CSS coding language. This was the first course I have taken in web-design, providing me with hands-on opportunity in a field I hope to work in the future.
  • PSY274: Social Psychology
    • I enjoyed Social Psychology because it explained many of human's innate processes which I find to be very interesting. Understanding psychology is important because it explains why humans do certain things and act the way they do.
  • SOC101: Introduction to Sociology
    • I took this class during my first semester at S.U. and it is still one of my favorites. I enjoy sociology because it allows us to question society and the institutions set in place.
